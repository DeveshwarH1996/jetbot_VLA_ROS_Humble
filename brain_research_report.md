# The JetBot "Brain": LLM/VLA/World-Model Options for a Jetson Nano 4GB Client + Dual RTX 3090 Ti Server

Robot profile assumed throughout: wheeled differential-drive base, monocular CSI camera (no depth/LiDAR for perception), Jetson Nano 4GB doing reactive/safety control, a dual-GPU (2x RTX 3090 Ti, 24GB each) workstation doing the heavy inference and returning actions/waypoints. No arm, no gripper.

---

## 1. General LLM/VLM Approach

**Gemini Robotics / Robotics-ER**: Closed-weight, API/trusted-tester access only — Robotics-ER 1.6 is reachable via Google AI Studio and the Gemini API, and full Gemini Robotics access is restricted to a trusted-tester cohort (Boston Dynamics, Agility, etc.), so it **cannot be self-hosted on a local GPU workstation** ([DeepMind ER page](https://deepmind.google/models/gemini-robotics/gemini-robotics-er/); [Gemini API robotics overview](https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/robotics-overview); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemini_Robotics)). It's genuinely strong at *grounded* output — 2D pointing, affordance-aware grasp points, motion trajectories, with ER 1.6 improving spatial reasoning/pointing over prior versions ([DeepMind ER](https://deepmind.google/models/gemini-robotics/gemini-robotics-er/)). A separate "Gemini Robotics On-Device" model is built for local inference, fine-tunable from 50-100 demos, but is also gated to trusted testers, not open weights ([Google Blog](https://deepmind.google/blog/gemini-robotics-on-device-brings-ai-to-local-robotic-devices/)).

**Gemma 3**: Open-weight (1B/4B/12B/27B, vision from 4B up), runs comfortably on one 3090 Ti even at 27B with quantization ([Roboflow](https://blog.roboflow.com/gemma-3/)). No published robotics/grounded-action benchmarks, however — it's a general VQA/OCR model, not evaluated on action generation; robot use would require building a VLA fine-tuning layer on top (analogous to how SigLIP+Gemma backbones are used elsewhere).

**Qwen2.5-VL**: Open-weight (Apache-licensed variants), self-hostable — 7B fits on one 24GB GPU, 72B needs quantization or dual-GPU (feasible on a 2x RTX 3090 Ti workstation) ([overview](https://debuggercafe.com/qwen2-5-vl/)). Its derivative **Qwen-RobotNav**, built on the Qwen VL backbone, reports actual grounded-navigation numbers: 72.1% success on R2R and 76.5% on RxR panoramic VLN-CE ([MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/16/meet-qwen-robotsuite-three-embodied-ai-models-for-vla-manipulation-video-world-modeling-and-navigation/)) — the strongest concrete "action, not caption" evidence found for any open-weight family.

**LLaVA-based**: OpenVLA (7B, LLaVA-style backbone) is open-weight, needs ~15GB bf16 / ~7GB at 4-bit, runs ~6Hz on an RTX-4090-class GPU — compatible with a single 3090 Ti. It beats the 55B closed RT-2-X by 16.5 points on BridgeData V2 despite being 7x smaller ([arXiv 2406.09246](https://arxiv.org/html/2406.09246v3)) — but manipulation-focused, not wheeled-nav. **Social-LLaVA** (LLaVA-v1.5-7B fine-tune) targets exactly JetBot-style differential-drive navigation, deployable on a Jetson AGX Orin at 9.2 FPS, scoring 4.06/5 human-rated social-navigation reasoning vs GPT-4V's 3.33 ([Emergent Mind](https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/social-llava)) — human-rubric evaluated, weaker quantitative grounding than a success-rate benchmark.

**Takeaway**: Qwen2.5-VL/Qwen-RobotNav and OpenVLA/Social-LLaVA are the only options that are both self-hostable on a local GPU workstation and have measured grounded-action benchmarks; Gemini Robotics is the strongest at grounded action but is closed and API-gated, making it a poor fit for a self-hosted project.

## 2. Dedicated VLA Models

**Manipulation-focused VLAs dominate the field, and none of them target wheeled bases.** OpenVLA (7B, open weights) needs ~15-16GB VRAM for bf16 inference, ~24GB for LoRA fine-tuning (fits one 3090 Ti), but 40-80GB for full fine-tuning ([arXiv:2406.09246](https://arxiv.org/html/2406.09246v2), [openvla-oft](https://openvla-oft.github.io/), [GitHub #311](https://github.com/openvla/openvla/issues/311)) — trained purely on Open X-Embodiment single/dual-arm manipulation data. Physical Intelligence's π0/π0-FAST (open, `openpi` release): ~8GB inference, ~22.5GB LoRA, ~70GB+ full fine-tune, built on ALOHA/DROID arm data — manipulation-only ([pi.website/blog/openpi](https://www.pi.website/blog/openpi), [GitHub](https://github.com/Physical-Intelligence/openpi)). Successor π0.5 adds mobile-*manipulator* household tasks (base+arm), still not pure wheeled navigation ([pi.website/blog/pi05](https://www.pi.website/blog/pi05), [arXiv:2504.16054](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.16054)). NVIDIA GR00T N1/N1.5 (open, 3B+550M diffusion head): ~25GB default fine-tune, ~16GB with LoRA, targets humanoid manipulation ([HF blog](https://huggingface.co/blog/nvidia/gr00t-n1-5-so101-tuning), [arXiv:2503.14734](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.14734)). Octo's own authors flag navigation/mobile use as unaddressed future work ([arXiv:2405.12213](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.12213), [octo-models.github.io](https://octo-models.github.io/)). RT-2 was **never open-sourced** — only smaller RT-X models are on HuggingFace ([RT-2](https://robotics-transformer2.github.io/), [RT-X](https://robotics-transformer-x.github.io/)). SpatialVLA, CogACT, RoboVLMs are research variants in the same OpenVLA/OXE lineage — manipulation-only.

**Navigation-specific alternatives exist and fit better.** OmniVLA builds on the OpenVLA 7B backbone but explicitly trains for wheeled robots (FrodoBots, VizBot) and quadrupeds ([arXiv:2509.19480](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.19480)) — same VRAM profile as OpenVLA, LoRA-tunable on one 3090 Ti. InternVLA-N1 claims wheeled-to-humanoid cross-embodiment generalization. NaVILA (8B) targets legged robots, not wheeled. Berkeley's GNM/ViNT/NoMaD are open-source, purpose-built, already deployed on wheeled TurtleBot2/LoCoBot, and far smaller than a "VLA" — they don't need a 48GB workstation at all ([GitHub](https://github.com/robodhruv/visualnav-transformer)).

**Bottom line**: The flagship VLAs (OpenVLA, π0/FAST, GR00T) are computationally deployable/LoRA-fine-tunable on the dual-3090 Ti box, but their action heads output arm/gripper poses, not (v, ω) wheel commands — there's no pretrained transfer benefit for a differential-drive base. OmniVLA is the most directly relevant flagship-scale match; GNM/ViNT/NoMaD are the pragmatic, proven, lightweight fallback (detailed in section 4).

## 3. World Models / WAMs for the Safety Layer

The research splits into two very different weight classes.

**Frontier video-generation world models** — NVIDIA's Cosmos family (Cosmos-Predict, Cosmos-Transfer, Cosmos Policy) explicitly targets predicting future world states/robot actions and is used by 1X, Figure, Agility, Skild ([nvidia.com/cosmos](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/ai/cosmos/), [arXiv 2501.03575](https://arxiv.org/html/2501.03575v1), [therobotreport.com](https://www.therobotreport.com/nvidia-adds-cosmos-policy-world-foundation-models/)). DeepMind's Genie 2/3 is described by DeepMind as useful for "training, safety tests, and scenario design" ([deepmind.google/genie-2](https://deepmind.google/blog/genie-2-a-large-scale-foundation-world-model/)); Waymo adapted Genie 3 for edge-case simulation ([fenxi.fr](https://fenxi.fr/en/blog/genie-3-google-deepmind-world-model-2/)). 1X's own "world model" work predicts action outcomes for exactly the safety-anticipation purpose this project wants ([1x.tech](https://www.1x.tech/discover/redwood-ai-world-model), [venturebeat.com](https://venturebeat.com/ai/1x-releases-generative-world-models-to-train-robots)). But these are large diffusion/autoregressive video models — Cosmos-Predict2.5's 2B variant alone needs ~32.5GB VRAM and NVIDIA recommends H100/A100-80GB-class hardware; the 14B variant needs ~49GB and multiview setups want 8×80GB GPUs ([docs.nvidia.com/cosmos](https://docs.nvidia.com/cosmos/latest/prerequisites.html), [HF Cosmos-Predict2.5-2B](https://huggingface.co/nvidia/Cosmos-Predict2.5-2B)). They generate video frame-by-frame, not at the 10-50Hz a collision-veto layer needs, and are built for offline data generation/training, not an inline real-time shield.

**Model-based RL world models are the actually relevant branch.** DreamerV3's RSSM (tens of millions of parameters) trains on a single RTX 3090 and runs latent-space rollouts at 1000+ steps/sec ([guide](https://www.roboticscenter.ai/blog/world-models-getting-started-guide), [github.com/A-SHOJAEI/dreamerv3-robotic-control](https://github.com/A-SHOJAEI/dreamerv3-robotic-control)). Directly on point: SafeDreamer (ICLR 2024) pairs a Dreamer-style world model with a Lagrangian safety critic to achieve near-zero constraint violations by imagining trajectories forward before acting ([arXiv 2307.07176](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.07176)); "Approximate Model-Based Shielding" implements the same shield pattern explicitly ([arXiv 2308.00707](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2308.00707)).

**Verdict**: Replacing the rule-based `predictive_governor` veto with a Cosmos/Genie-class world model is impractical — wrong tool, wrong latency profile, and it would reintroduce the network round-trip fragility that the heartbeat fix (commit `202e7a3`) just addressed if run remotely on a GPU server. A compact DreamerV3/SafeDreamer-style latent world model, trained on this robot's own camera/odometry/action logs and run locally on the Jetson (or distilled from training done on the GPU server), is realistic and matches published hobbyist-scale precedent.

## 4. Navigation-Specific Models vs. Manipulation VLAs

**Manipulation VLA transfer to navigation is not direct.** RT-2 and OpenVLA train almost entirely on tabletop/gripper manipulation data (Open X-Embodiment: fixed-base arms, gripper end-effector actions); RT-2 "set the architectural template" that later mobile-robot work borrowed rather than solving navigation itself ([OpenVLA arXiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2406.09246), [CodeSOTA VLA landscape](https://www.codesota.com/robotics)). Their action space (6-DoF end-effector pose + gripper) has no native mapping to differential-drive wheel velocities, and their training distributions contain almost no ground-navigation trajectories — "transfer" would require re-training/fine-tuning on navigation data anyway, at which point the manipulation prior buys little.

**A better-fitting class: Berkeley's General Navigation Models — GNM → ViNT → NoMaD.** Purpose-built, embodiment-agnostic, monocular-RGB navigation policies:
- **GNM** (ICRA 2023): a shared goal-conditioned policy trained across many real ground robots so sensing/actuation differences wash out ([project page](https://general-navigation-models.github.io/)).
- **ViNT** (CoRL 2023): a Transformer foundation model trained on hundreds of hours of cross-platform navigation logs (RECON, TartanDrive, SCAND, GoStanford2, SACSoN/HuRoN), predicting normalized waypoint actions from a single forward RGB camera; generalizes zero-shot and can be prompt-tuned onto GPS/route goals ([arXiv 2306.14846](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.14846), [GitHub](https://github.com/robodhruv/visualnav-transformer)).
- **NoMaD** (2023): reuses the ViNT encoder but replaces the action head with a diffusion policy, unifying exploration and goal-reaching in one small model — reported to need 15x less compute than prior SOTA while improving success rate >25% ([arXiv 2310.07896](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2310.07896)).

Sensors: all three need only a wide-angle monocular RGB camera plus odometry — no LiDAR/depth required, matching this robot exactly ([GitHub README](https://github.com/robodhruv/visualnav-transformer)). Deployment: the reference implementation runs onboard an NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano on a LoCoBot, and has been ported to Jackal, TurtleBot2, Vizbot, and DJI Tello — hardware weaker than a 2x RTX 3090 Ti workstation, so it would run with enormous headroom, trivially real-time.

Separately, classic VLN (Vision-and-Language Navigation, e.g. R2R-style) models historically assumed panoramic/discretized simulator viewpoints, a poor match for a single CSI camera; newer 2025-2026 work closes that gap for real monocular robots — MonoDream (monocular-only VLN via panoramic dreaming, [arXiv 2508.02549](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2508.02549)), and edge-deployed variants DyGeoVLN and AgentVLN, targeting Jetson-class onboard/edge inference with a single RGB stream ([arXiv 2603.21269](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.21269v1), [arXiv 2603.17670](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.17670)).

**Assessment**: For this robot's exact architecture (monocular CSI camera, no depth/LiDAR, Jetson doing only reactive control, GPU workstation as remote "brain" returning actions), the GNM/ViNT/NoMaD lineage — or a language-conditioned VLN descendant like MonoDream/DyGeoVLN — is a substantially more realistic fit than adapting OpenVLA/RT-2: correct sensor assumptions, correct action space (waypoints/velocities, not gripper poses), a proven small-model footprint the 3090Ti pair runs with large margin, and existing precedent of running server-side with cheap client-side execution.

## RECOMMENDATION

**Try ViNT (or NoMaD) first, running server-side on a GPU workstation, with the Jetson doing only image capture, action execution, and the safety veto.** Do not start with OpenVLA, GR00T, π0, or any Open-X-Embodiment VLA, and do not start with a video-generation world model (Cosmos/Genie). Reasoning:

- **This is a navigation robot, not a manipulator.** Every flagship VLA (OpenVLA, π0/π0-FAST, GR00T, Octo, RT-2/RT-X) outputs arm/gripper poses learned from tabletop manipulation data. None of them have a native action space for (v, ω) wheel commands, and none show navigation-relevant training data in their pretraining mix. Adopting one means throwing away its pretrained prior and fine-tuning from near-scratch on your own nav data — at which point you paid the VRAM/complexity cost of a 7B+ arm-centric model for zero transfer benefit. This is the single clearest, least-hedged conclusion across all four findings.
- **ViNT/NoMaD are purpose-built for exactly this sensor/action profile**: monocular RGB + odometry in, waypoints/velocities out — no LiDAR or depth needed, matching this robot's stated sensor loadout exactly. They are proven on wheeled robots (TurtleBot2, Jackal, LoCoBot, Vizbot), already run in real time on hardware weaker than a 2x RTX 3090 Ti workstation (Jetson Orin Nano), and have a track record of running server-side with a thin client — architecturally identical to this project's Jetson-client/GPU-server split.
- **If more capability is wanted later**, OmniVLA is the correct escalation path, not OpenVLA — it's the one flagship-scale VLA actually trained on wheeled-robot data (FrodoBots, VizBot) on the same OpenVLA backbone, so it's LoRA-fine-tunable on a single 3090 Ti when ViNT/NoMaD's capacity is outgrown. Qwen-RobotNav is a second reasonable escalation if language-conditioned instruction-following (not just goal-image navigation) becomes a requirement — it has the best published grounded-navigation success-rate numbers (72.1% R2R / 76.5% RxR) of any open-weight model surveyed.
- **For the safety/veto layer, do not use a large world model.** Cosmos and Genie are the wrong tool: they need 32-80GB+ VRAM even at inference and generate video frame-by-frame, far too slow for a 10-50Hz collision-veto loop, and running them remotely reintroduces the exact network round-trip fragility the heartbeat fix (commit `202e7a3`) was written to eliminate. Instead, train a small DreamerV3/SafeDreamer-style latent world model (tens of millions of parameters, trains on one RTX 3090, does 1000+ imagined rollout-steps/sec) on this robot's own camera+odometry+action logs, and run it locally on the Jetson, keeping the existing rule-based `predictive_governor` as a documented fallback until the learned shield is validated in practice — don't rip it out on day one.
- **Skip Gemini Robotics entirely for self-hosted use.** It's the strongest model at grounded action generation of anything surveyed, but it's API/trusted-tester-gated with no path to self-hosting on a local GPU workstation — not a fit for a project built around local inference on owned hardware.

**Concrete first step**: stand up ViNT (or NoMaD if diffusion-based exploration+goal-reaching is wanted in one model) server-side on the GPU workstation using the existing `visualnav-transformer` codebase and pretrained checkpoints, feed it the JetBot's monocular camera stream over the network link to the GPU workstation, and validate waypoint-following through the current motor driver before investing in any VLA fine-tuning or world-model safety layer.
